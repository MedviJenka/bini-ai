<h3>M-2 25-0739 Motion Approving an Agreement Pursuant to Invitation to Bid No. 476-1 for Crossing Guard Services - GTOPSS LP - $3,165,372</h3>
<p><strong>Staff Presentation:</strong> Glenn Markel, Chief Procurement Officer; Police Chief Bill Schultz; City Manager clarified this is a 2-year contract totaling $3,165,372. The city has customarily covered crossing guard costs for several years. Contract covers 18 schools (all public schools) with services both morning and afternoon.</p>
<p><strong>Public Speaker:</strong> Mr. King questioned why the city pays $3.1 million for crossing guards instead of the school board, and why private schools are excluded from the program when they also receive public tax dollars through state voucher programs.</p>
<p><strong>Council Discussion:</strong> Commissioner Sorensen acknowledged the fair point about private schools and indicated staff is evaluating schools (public and private) for traffic issues and may consider expanding crossing guard services. Police Chief explained private schools typically hire their own off-duty police details for security and crossing services.</p>
<p><strong>Motion:</strong> Motion to approve M-2 crossing guard services agreement. <strong>Vote:</strong> Carried Unanimously</p>
